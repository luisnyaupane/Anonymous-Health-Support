❓ Myth Buster & Facts

Learn the truth about reproductive health and clear common misconceptions.

🌀 Myth: You can't get pregnant during your period.

Many people believe menstruation is a safe time. But sperm can live inside the body for up to 5 days, and early ovulation can lead to pregnancy.

✅ Fact: You *can* get pregnant if your cycle is irregular or ovulation is early.

🧼 Myth: Washing genitals with Dettol or soap prevents infections.

Using harsh chemicals can disrupt the natural flora and cause irritation.

✅ Fact: Use only clean water or mild pH-balanced intimate washes.

🔴 Myth: Period blood is dirty and impure.

This is a common cultural stigma in Nepal. Menstruation is a natural, healthy process — not shameful.

✅ Fact: Period blood is clean and just like any other blood — it’s not impure.

🍌 Myth: Masturbation causes weakness or infertility.

There is no scientific evidence linking masturbation to any physical harm.

✅ Fact: Masturbation is safe and normal. It doesn’t affect fertility or health.

🗣️ Myth: Talking about sex with youth will encourage them to do it early.

Many parents and teachers fear that sex education will lead to early sexual activity.

✅ Fact: Honest, age-appropriate sex education *delays* sexual activity and promotes safety.

😷 Myth: Only people who sleep around get STIs.

STIs can affect anyone, even those in long-term relationships, due to past exposure or lack of protection.

✅ Fact: STIs don’t discriminate. Protection and testing are key.
