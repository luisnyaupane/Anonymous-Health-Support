⚖️ Reproductive Health Rights

Understand your rights — backed by Nepali law, WHO, and global human rights frameworks.

🇳🇵 Nepal Constitution & Laws

  • Article 38 (2): Every woman shall have the right to safe motherhood and reproductive health.
  • Safe Abortion: Legal up to 12 weeks on request, up to 18 weeks in cases of rape/incest, and anytime with doctor’s advice if the mother’s life is in danger.
  • Consent is mandatory: No one — including spouse or parent — can force reproductive decisions on another.
  • Adolescents (under 18): Have the right to reproductive health education and confidential services.

🧾 Source: Nepal Law Commission

🌐 WHO Guidelines on Reproductive Rights

  • Everyone has the right to make informed decisions about reproductive health — without discrimination, coercion, or violence.
  • Access to contraception, maternal care, STI testing and education must be universal.
  • Privacy must be respected — including for young people and LGBTQ+ individuals.

📚 Source: WHO: Sexual & Reproductive Health

🕊️ Universal Human Rights (UN)

  • Reproductive rights are part of the right to life, dignity, and health (Article 25 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights).
  • Right to decide freely the number and spacing of children (ICPD, 1994).
  • Right to access reproductive healthcare regardless of age, caste, gender, or marital status.

🌍 Reference: OHCHR – UN Human Rights

🧠 Your Rights in Simple Language

  • You have the right to say NO — to sex, pregnancy, or medical tests.
  • You can access condoms, birth control, and checkups — even without telling anyone.
  • You have the right to learn about your body and ask questions anonymously.
  • No one has the right to shame you for your reproductive health choices.
